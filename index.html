You may visit Sri Lanka at any time of year. Because it is a tropical nation, anticipate sunlight and beautiful sky almost all year, even during the wet season. Sri Lanka has two monsoon seasons, and the climate varies across the nation. The northeast monsoon occurs from December to March. The southwest monsoon rains extend between June and October. You can adjust your plans according to the season with our experts’ help.

Sri Lanka is a welcoming and secure destination, offering both safety and breathtaking landscapes. With the country at peace and more visitors discovering its beauty, now is the best time to explore. From beautiful beaches to lush hills, every part of Sri Lanka leaves a lasting impression. We promise, once you visit, you’ll want to come back for more!

Yes, you do! Your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond the end of your trip to Sri Lanka to be eligible to visit. Before your trip, you may apply for a visa online www.eta.gov.lk

The tropics can be quite humid. Relaxed-fit attire would be advisable. However, when you reach the hills you may want to add a layer on. A few cardigans may be good to pack.

When visiting religious places, you will need to cover your shoulders and knees. A scarf may be a smart choice to pack. Be a Polite Traveler: When visiting religious places, always remember to, take off your caps and other headgear and remove your shoes wherever required. Your travel host will be your guide to help you with customs.

You’ll need plenty of sunscreen (or sun-protective clothes) and bug repellant. Expect heat and humidity, but dress warmly if you’re going to the hill region. Antiseptic hand gels or wipes will be useful for families with small children.

Sri Lanka’s currency is the Rupee (Rs), available in denominations of 20, 50, 100, 500, 1000, and 5000. When exchanging money, it’s advisable to break larger notes, especially Rs 5000, as smaller businesses may have difficulty providing change.

Credit cards, particularly Visa and MasterCard, are widely accepted throughout the country, including in smaller towns, while Amex is accepted to a lesser extent. For convenience, using an internationally valid credit card is recommended whenever possible. Keep in mind that exchange rates from online currency converters serve only as a guideline; the actual rate applied will be the prevailing bank rate at the time of the transaction.

Planning a trip to Sri Lanka can be challenging, especially when it comes to logistics. As a round-tour destination, getting around can be tricky since public transport is not well-networked, and reserving seats can be difficult. A travel agency with established local connections can make your journey seamless by arranging reliable transportation, ensuring smooth transfers, and providing expert guidance on the best routes and experiences.

At Walkers Tours, we take care of every detail, ensuring a hassle-free journey so you can focus on enjoying the beauty of Sri Lanka. From transportation to personalized experiences, we’re here to plan every step of the way!
